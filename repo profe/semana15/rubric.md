# Milestone 3: Final Project Presentation Rubric

## Data Analytics Course - Universidad Cooperativa de Colombia

---

## Overview

This rubric evaluates the complete end-to-end data analytics project, from initial problem framing through machine learning modeling to actionable business recommendations. Students demonstrate mastery of the CRISP-DM methodology using real datasets from datos.gov.co.

**Total Points:** 100
**Presentation Time:** 20 minutes + 5 minutes Q&A

---

## Grading Scale

| Grade | Percentage | Description |
|-------|------------|-------------|
| Exemplary | 90-100% | Exceeds expectations; demonstrates mastery and creativity |
| Proficient | 75-89% | Meets all requirements with competence and clarity |
| Developing | 60-74% | Shows understanding but has gaps in execution |
| Beginning | Below 60% | Significant gaps; does not meet minimum requirements |

---

## Category 1: Business Understanding (10 points)

*Assesses the student's ability to frame the analytics problem in business context.*

### Exemplary (9-10 points)
- Problem statement is compelling, specific, and clearly tied to real-world impact
- Stakeholders are identified with their specific needs and decision-making context
- Project objectives are SMART (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound)
- Demonstrates deep understanding of the Colombian context and why this problem matters
- Success criteria are clearly defined and measurable

### Proficient (7.5-8.9 points)
- Problem statement is clear and relevant
- Key stakeholders are identified with general needs described
- Project objectives are specific and achievable
- Shows awareness of the broader context
- Success criteria are present but may lack specificity

### Developing (6-7.4 points)
- Problem statement exists but is vague or overly broad
- Stakeholders are mentioned but their needs are not clearly articulated
- Objectives are present but not well-defined
- Limited connection to real-world application
- Success criteria are missing or unclear

### Beginning (0-5.9 points)
- No clear problem statement or business context
- Stakeholders not identified
- Objectives are missing or irrelevant
- No connection to practical application
- Cannot articulate why this project matters

**Score: ___ / 10**

## Category 2: Data Understanding (15 points)

*Evaluates the quality of exploratory data analysis and pattern discovery.*

### Exemplary (13.5-15 points)
- Comprehensive EDA covering all relevant variables
- Insightful pattern discovery with clear explanations of findings
- Statistical summaries are accurate and well-interpreted
- Distributions, correlations, and outliers are thoroughly analyzed
- Data quality issues are identified and documented with specific examples
- Visualizations during EDA are appropriate and informative
- Demonstrates curiosity; asked and answered interesting questions about the data

### Proficient (11.25-13.4 points)
- Solid EDA covering key variables
- Patterns are identified and reasonably explained
- Statistical summaries are accurate
- Major distributions and correlations are explored
- Data quality issues are noted
- Visualizations support the analysis
- Shows engagement with the data beyond surface-level exploration

### Developing (9-11.24 points)
- Basic EDA performed but missing key analyses
- Some patterns identified but explanations are shallow
- Statistical summaries present but may contain errors
- Limited exploration of distributions or correlations
- Data quality issues partially addressed
- Visualizations are present but may not be optimal
- Analysis feels mechanical rather than investigative

### Beginning (0-8.9 points)
- Minimal or no EDA performed
- No meaningful patterns identified
- Statistical summaries missing or incorrect
- No analysis of distributions, correlations, or outliers
- Data quality issues ignored
- Visualizations missing or inappropriate
- No evidence of genuine data exploration

**Score: ___ / 15**

## Category 3: Data Preparation (10 points)

*Assesses data cleaning, transformation, and feature engineering decisions.*

### Exemplary (9-10 points)
- Comprehensive data cleaning with clear documentation of all decisions
- Missing value handling strategy is appropriate and well-justified
- Feature engineering demonstrates creativity and domain understanding
- Transformations (encoding, scaling, etc.) are correctly applied
- Train/test split is appropriate and prevents data leakage
- Data preparation pipeline is reproducible and well-documented
- Explains the "why" behind each preparation decision

### Proficient (7.5-8.9 points)
- Adequate data cleaning performed
- Missing values handled appropriately
- Some feature engineering present
- Standard transformations correctly applied
- Proper train/test split implemented
- Preparation steps are documented
- Decisions are justified

### Developing (6-7.4 points)
- Basic cleaning performed but incomplete
- Missing value handling present but may not be optimal
- Little to no feature engineering
- Some transformation errors present
- Train/test split exists but may have issues
- Documentation is sparse
- Limited justification for decisions

### Beginning (0-5.9 points)
- Minimal or no data cleaning
- Missing values not addressed or handled incorrectly
- No feature engineering
- Transformations missing or incorrect
- Data leakage present or no proper split
- No documentation of preparation steps
- Cannot explain preparation decisions

**Score: ___ / 10**

## Category 4: Modeling (20 points)

*Evaluates model selection, implementation, and comparison.*

### Exemplary (18-20 points)
- Multiple appropriate algorithms selected with clear rationale for each
- Implementation is correct with proper use of libraries
- Hyperparameter tuning is performed and documented
- Model comparison is thorough and uses appropriate criteria
- Demonstrates understanding of algorithm assumptions and limitations
- Can explain how the chosen model(s) work at a conceptual level
- Code is clean, organized, and reproducible

### Proficient (15-17.9 points)
- At least two appropriate algorithms implemented
- Implementation is correct
- Some hyperparameter exploration performed
- Models are compared using relevant metrics
- Shows understanding of chosen algorithms
- Can explain basic model concepts
- Code works and is reasonably organized

### Developing (12-14.9 points)
- At least one appropriate algorithm implemented
- Implementation works but may have minor issues
- Limited hyperparameter tuning
- Comparison is superficial or missing
- Basic understanding of algorithms shown
- Difficulty explaining model concepts
- Code works but is disorganized

### Beginning (0-11.9 points)
- Inappropriate or no model implemented
- Significant implementation errors
- No hyperparameter consideration
- No model comparison
- Does not understand chosen algorithm(s)
- Cannot explain how the model works
- Code does not run or produces incorrect results

**Score: ___ / 20**

## Category 5: Evaluation (15 points)

*Assesses proper use of evaluation metrics, validation techniques, and interpretation.*

### Exemplary (13.5-15 points)
- Appropriate evaluation metrics selected and justified for the problem type
- Cross-validation or proper holdout validation implemented
- Results are correctly interpreted in business context
- Confusion matrix (if classification) analyzed with precision, recall, F1 discussion
- Overfitting/underfitting assessment is present
- Model limitations are honestly discussed
- Statistical significance or confidence intervals considered where appropriate

### Proficient (11.25-13.4 points)
- Appropriate metrics used
- Validation strategy is sound
- Results are interpreted correctly
- Standard evaluation outputs are present and discussed
- Some consideration of overfitting
- Limitations acknowledged
- Interpretation is reasonable

### Developing (9-11.24 points)
- Metrics present but may not be optimal for the problem
- Basic validation performed
- Interpretation is present but may be superficial
- Evaluation outputs shown but not thoroughly analyzed
- Overfitting not addressed
- Limitations not discussed
- Interpretation may contain errors

### Beginning (0-8.9 points)
- Inappropriate or missing metrics
- No proper validation
- Results misinterpreted or not interpreted
- Missing evaluation outputs
- No understanding of overfitting
- No acknowledgment of limitations
- Fundamental errors in evaluation

**Score: ___ / 15**

## Category 6: Insights & Recommendations (15 points)

*Evaluates the quality and actionability of findings and recommendations.*

### Exemplary (13.5-15 points)
- Insights are non-obvious and derived directly from the analysis
- At least 3 specific, actionable recommendations provided
- Recommendations are SMART and tied to evidence from the project
- Clear connection between findings and proposed actions
- Potential impact is quantified where possible
- Implementation considerations (cost, timeline, risks) are addressed
- Recommendations are appropriate for the identified stakeholders
- Demonstrates critical thinking about what the data does and does not support

### Proficient (11.25-13.4 points)
- Insights are relevant and supported by analysis
- 2-3 actionable recommendations provided
- Recommendations are specific and evidence-based
- Connection between findings and actions is clear
- Some consideration of impact
- Basic implementation awareness
- Appropriate for audience

### Developing (9-11.24 points)
- Insights are present but may be obvious or weakly supported
- Recommendations exist but lack specificity
- Weak connection between analysis and recommendations
- Impact not quantified
- Implementation not considered
- May not be appropriate for stakeholders
- Recommendations feel generic

### Beginning (0-8.9 points)
- No meaningful insights derived
- Recommendations missing or not actionable
- No connection to analysis
- No consideration of impact
- No implementation awareness
- Not appropriate for any stakeholder
- Recommendations not supported by evidence

**Score: ___ / 15**

## Category 7: Storytelling & Presentation (15 points)

*Assesses narrative structure, visual quality, and delivery.*

### Exemplary (13.5-15 points)
- **Narrative:** Compelling story arc from problem to solution; audience engagement is high
- **Visuals:** All visualizations are clear, professional, and effectively communicate insights
- **Slides:** Clean design, appropriate amount of text, consistent formatting
- **Delivery:** Confident, clear speech; maintains eye contact; manages time perfectly
- **Q&A:** Handles questions with poise; demonstrates depth of understanding
- **Technical fluency:** Seamlessly navigates between slides, code, and demos
- Leaves audience with a clear understanding of the project's value

### Proficient (11.25-13.4 points)
- **Narrative:** Logical flow; story is present but may not be compelling
- **Visuals:** Visualizations are clear and appropriate
- **Slides:** Professional appearance with minor issues
- **Delivery:** Clear presentation with occasional hesitation
- **Q&A:** Answers most questions adequately
- **Technical fluency:** Handles transitions reasonably well
- Audience understands the main points

### Developing (9-11.24 points)
- **Narrative:** Structure exists but flow is choppy
- **Visuals:** Visualizations are present but may be cluttered or suboptimal
- **Slides:** Inconsistent design or too much text
- **Delivery:** Reads from slides; lacks confidence
- **Q&A:** Struggles with some questions
- **Technical fluency:** Technical difficulties or awkward transitions
- Key messages are unclear

### Beginning (0-8.9 points)
- **Narrative:** No clear structure; difficult to follow
- **Visuals:** Missing, confusing, or inappropriate visualizations
- **Slides:** Unprofessional or unreadable
- **Delivery:** Inaudible, unclear, or significantly over/under time
- **Q&A:** Cannot answer basic questions about the project
- **Technical fluency:** Significant technical failures
- Audience cannot understand the project
