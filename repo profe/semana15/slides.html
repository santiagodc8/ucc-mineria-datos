Open with Impact

DO: Lead with the Problem

  • "In 2024, Colombian hospitals faced a 23% increase in patient readmissions..."
  • "Municipal budget allocation affects 2.3 million citizens in Bogota..."
  • Start with a compelling statistic or question
  • Make the audience care immediately

DON'T: Lead with Technical Details

  • "We used pandas to load a CSV file..."
  • "Our dataset has 50,000 rows and 25 columns..."
  • Starting with tools or data structure
  • Jumping into methodology first

Visual Excellence

Clarity First

  • One message per chart
  • Clear titles that state the insight
  • Labeled axes with units
  • Appropriate chart types

Consistent Design

  • Same color scheme throughout
  • Consistent font sizes
  • Aligned elements
  • Professional formatting

Meaningful Highlights

  • Emphasize key data points
  • Use annotations
  • Show trends clearly
  • Include context (benchmarks)
Pro Tip: Before showing a visualization, state what the audience should see. "This chart shows that sales peak in December..." guides their attention.

Making Recommendations Actionable

The SMART Framework

  • Specific - Clear and detailed
  • Measurable - Quantifiable outcomes
  • Achievable - Realistic to implement
  • Relevant - Tied to business goals
  • Time-bound - With a timeline

Example Transformation

Weak

"The city should improve transportation."

Strong

"Add 3 bus routes to District 5 by Q2 2025, reducing average commute time by 15 minutes for 12,000 residents."

Q&A Preparation

Anticipate, Prepare, Deliver

Anticipate These Questions

Technical Questions

  • Why did you choose this model over alternatives?
  • How did you handle missing values?
  • What was your train/test split ratio?
  • How would you improve the model?
  • What are the model's limitations?

Business Questions

  • Who are the main stakeholders?
  • How much would this cost to implement?
  • What's the expected ROI?
  • What are the risks of your recommendations?
  • How did you validate your findings?

Handling Difficult Questions

"I Don't Know"

  • It's okay to say it
  • Offer to follow up
  • Share related knowledge
  • Never make up answers

Clarifying

  • "Could you elaborate?"
  • "Do you mean X or Y?"
  • Ensure you understand
  • Paraphrase the question

Redirecting

  • "That's beyond our scope"
  • Acknowledge the point
  • Suggest future research
  • Stay focused on project
Golden Rule: It's better to confidently say "I don't know, but I would investigate by..." than to guess and undermine your credibility.

How You Present Matters

Presentation Best Practices

Before the Presentation

  • Practice at least 3 times
  • Time yourself (stay within 20 min)
  • Test all technical demos
  • Prepare backup slides
  • Have slides accessible offline
  • Know your first 2 sentences by heart

During the Presentation

  • Make eye contact with audience
  • Speak slowly and clearly
  • Pause after key points
  • Don't read from slides
  • Use the slides as prompts
  • Show enthusiasm for your findings

Common Pitfalls to Avoid

Content Issues

  • Too much technical jargon
  • Showing code without context
  • Cluttered visualizations
  • No clear call to action
  • Ignoring limitations
  • Recommendations without evidence

Delivery Issues

  • Reading directly from slides
  • Going over time
  • Speaking too fast when nervous
  • Not practicing transitions
  • Apologizing for your work
  • Defensive during Q&A

Pre-Presentation Checklist

Technical

  • Slides load correctly
  • All images display
  • Code snippets are readable
  • Power BI dashboard works
  • Notebook runs without errors
  • Backup on USB or cloud

Content

  • Clear problem statement
  • Data source cited
  • EDA findings highlighted
  • Model metrics explained
  • At least 3 recommendations
  • Limitations acknowledged
