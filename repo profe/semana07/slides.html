What Your EDA Should Include

Data Cleaning

  • Missing values handled
  • Data types corrected
  • Duplicates removed
  • String inconsistencies fixed
  • Before/after comparison

Univariate Analysis

  • Histograms for numeric vars
  • Boxplots for outlier detection
  • Bar charts for categorical vars
  • Summary statistics (mean, median, std)
  • Distribution descriptions

Bivariate Analysis

  • Correlation matrix + heatmap
  • Scatter plots for key pairs
  • Cross-tabulations
  • Grouped comparisons
  • Relationship interpretation

Insights

  • Key findings per variable
  • Outliers identified
  • Relationships explained
  • Connected to research questions
  • Limitations acknowledged
Scope: Milestone 1 covers data cleaning, univariate analysis, and bivariate/multivariate analysis. You should explore both individual variables and the relationships between them.

Analysis Quality: Good vs. Weak

Weak Analysis

  • "Here is a histogram"
  • "The mean is 145.3"
  • "There are some missing values"
  • "The data looks normal"

Describes what you see, no interpretation

Strong Analysis

  • "Right-skewed distribution (mean=145, median=98) suggests heavy users pulling the average up"
  • "23 outliers above Q3+1.5*IQR, likely industrial consumers"
  • "12% missing values in 'consumption' filled with median (robust to outliers)"

Interprets, explains, connects to context

Key Difference: Strong analysis tells a STORY. It does not just show charts; it explains what they mean and why it matters.

Peer Feedback Guidelines

Your feedback helps your classmates improve their projects

Good Feedback

  • "Your boxplot for income shows interesting outliers - did you investigate what those represent?"
  • "Consider using median instead of mean since the distribution is skewed"
  • "The before/after cleaning summary was very clear"
  • "I noticed your histogram uses 50 bins - that might be too many for 200 rows"

Unhelpful Feedback

  • "It was good" (too vague)
  • "I don't like the topic" (not constructive)
  • "The charts were boring" (subjective)
  • "You should change everything" (overwhelming)

Feedback Framework: WWW

  • What worked well? (Be specific)
  • What could be improved? (Be constructive)
  • What question do you have? (Show engagement)

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Data Cleaning

  • Dropping ALL rows with ANY missing value
  • Not documenting WHY you chose a fill strategy
  • Ignoring data types (e.g., dates stored as strings)
  • Not checking for duplicates at all
  • No before/after comparison

Analysis

  • Using mean for highly skewed distributions
  • Only showing charts without interpretation
  • Analyzing only 1-2 variables out of 15
  • Ignoring outliers completely
  • No connection to research questions
Remember: The goal is not to show every chart you can make. It is to show the most MEANINGFUL findings and explain what they tell us about the data.
